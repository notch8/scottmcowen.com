Last Lecture of Randy Pausch, Carnegie Mellon University, September 24, 2007

Video of lecture can be found at video.google.com

(Plus some of Scott McOwen’s additions)

His Dreams

Enabling the Dreams of others

Lessons Learned

Roles of Parents, Mentors, Friends, Colleague, and Students important.

-Pick your friends carefully – they influence more than you think.

-His parents: once when he was complaining to his mother how hard his studies were her response was “Oh you will do OK because when your father was your age he was fighting in WWII.” It put things in perspective for him.

-They also let him paint his room as he liked – let them do it.

-A mentor said – “Sell something worth while – like education.”

-His students taught him much.

-Tell your colleagues and friends about having fun. Decide if you are a Tigger or an Eeyore.

- Never lose the child like wonder. (Example is my 3 year old grand daughter getting excited about saying “Good-by” to the sun at sunset.

- Help others “It is fun to do the impossible, Professor Boy” – a colleague said. (Help others make money and you will.)

- Loyalty is a two-way street – Stand up for others

- Ignore what they say & Watch what they do

- Never Give Up – If you don’t get in a university or job you want keep trying.

- Tell the Truth – focus on the truth and reality. Ask, what is real, not phony?)

- Be Earnest - hip is short term

- Apologize when you screw up

- Focus on others, not yourself.

- Brick walls let us show our dedication. –They also limit the competition.

- Get a feedback loop; and listen to it! (It can be a chart that you have people fill out questionnaires or a person telling you)

- Show Gratitude – Thank people – thank everyone who gives a public talk – it is hard to get up front and takes time and energy.

- Don’t complain; just work harder. For example, Jackie Robinson.

- Be Good at something: it makes you valuable.

- Work hard… -Work hard and smart.

- Find the best in everybody; no matter how long you have to wait for them to show it.

- Be prepared: “luck” is where preparation meets opportunity.

- It is not about achieving your dreams but leading your life.

- If you lead a good life the dreams will come to you.

- Randy did this talk not for the audience, but his kids.

- Keep focused on what matters!!! Don’t let circumstances sway you.

- Leave a great legacy continuously. Don’t attempt to wait till the end, but you will find that you are constantly leaving your legacy – and that is what matters in the long run.
