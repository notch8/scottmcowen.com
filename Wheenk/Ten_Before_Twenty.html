10 Things I Wish I Knew Before I Was Twenty

Tina Seeliq (Stanford Univerity)

  1. Every problem is an opportunity for a creative solution. The bigger the problem the bigger the opportunity. (Example: $5 to $650 by selling their presentation time for recruit time. Then waited in line and putting air in tires.) No problem – no opportunity and no company.
  1. “The harder I work the luckier I get.” Travel, meet people, and put yourself out there. If you go somewhere and do not meet someone you probably have not met a new friend and probably missed making a million dollars.
  1. Find an intersection between your skills, your passions and the market. You can be a great fan of music but not being able to make a living at it.
  1. Try a lot of things and keep what works. If you are not failing enough you are not learning enough. You learn from your mistakes so take risks and learn. All successful people have had failures to get there. (Write a failure resume. Acknowledge what you learned from your failures.) Innovation is about experimentation. If you did not experiment big companies would do it.
  1. You do not have to wait to be anointed. You can be president at the start. You do not have to wait if it is your company. Entrepreneurs make their own business cards. Do the job and then you get the job. Take it, embrace it, and do not wait for someone to empower you. Just do it.
  1. It is a very small world. Do not burn bridges. You will bump into the same people the rest of your life in your industry and career.
  1. You can do it all, but just not at the same time. You have to think about your priorities. Change them regularly. These are the three things important now and then change them. Don’t change it every 5 minutes but not 5 years. Balance it over it over a longer period of time and catch it before it hits the floor. Look at the long perspective.
  1. It is the little things that matter the most. Look people in the eye. Shake their hands and say “please” and “thank you”. Write thank you notes to people.
  1. On a team it is important to make the others successful. The more you give the more you get back.
  1. Never miss an opportunity to be fabulous. This is not a dress rehearsal. Do your best. You are to give at least 100%. Don’t do just enough, but really do your very best work.
